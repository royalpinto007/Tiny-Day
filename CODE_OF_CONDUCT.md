# Code of Conduct

## Our standard

Tiny Day should be as calm and considerate to build as it is to use. Everyone
participating in this project is expected to help create a welcoming,
accessible, and harassment-free environment, regardless of background,
identity, experience, or ability.

Positive participation includes:

- Being respectful, patient, and specific in feedback.
- Assuming good intent while addressing the actual impact of words and actions.
- Welcoming questions and helping people learn without condescension.
- Respecting different needs, constraints, and communication styles.
- Accepting correction and repairing harm when mistakes happen.

Unacceptable behavior includes harassment, discriminatory language, personal
attacks, threats, sexualized attention, deliberate intimidation, publishing
private information, or repeatedly disrupting project spaces.

## Scope

This standard applies in issues, pull requests, discussions, release feedback,
and other spaces where someone is representing the Tiny Day community.

## Reporting and enforcement

Report conduct concerns privately to the repository owner through their GitHub
profile: <https://github.com/royalpinto007>. Do not open a public issue for a
sensitive report. Reports will be reviewed as promptly and confidentially as
practical.

Maintainers may edit or remove content and may warn, temporarily restrict, or
permanently ban participants whose behavior is harmful. Enforcement decisions
will consider context, impact, recurrence, and the safety of the community.

## Attribution

This policy is informed by the Contributor Covenant and the Mozilla Community
Participation Guidelines, adapted into a concise policy for this project.
